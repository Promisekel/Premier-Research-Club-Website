Critical Research Findings

  • Opportunity Gap: Only 14.0% of students got screened when given the opportunity, compared to 12.6% who took self-initiative, highlighting missed opportunities in healthcare delivery.
  • Access Bottleneck: 66.4% of students were given screening opportunities, but the conversion rate remains critically low, indicating systemic barriers beyond access.
  • Low Overall Uptake: With only 13.6% screening uptake rate, urgent public health interventions are needed to meet WHO recommendations.
  • Statistical Significance: Modified Poisson Regression reveals that opportunity provision is the primary predictor, but quality of counseling and follow-up mechanisms need improvement.
  • Awareness-Action Gap: Despite 39.8% awareness rate, the screening uptake remains low, suggesting knowledge doesn't automatically translate to health-seeking behavior.

Study Methodology

  • Study Design: Cross-sectional analytical study among female tertiary students in Hohoe Municipality, Ghana.
  • Sample Size: 354 participants recruited through systematic random sampling from three tertiary institutions.
  • Statistical Analysis: Modified Poisson Regression with robust standard errors (HC0) to estimate Risk Ratios rather than Odds Ratios.
  • Key Variables: Primary outcome - Cervical cancer screening uptake (Binary: Yes/No). Predictors include demographics, awareness variables, and knowledge domains.
  • Risk Ratio Interpretation: RR > 1 indicates higher likelihood of screening compared to reference group; RR < 1 indicates lower likelihood.
